Two things in life give you a dopamine hit: chocolate and getting the flag after days and nights of trying.

I'm an R&D engineer with 5 years of experience, and I've been doing CTFs for a few years on the side, mostly focused on binary exploitation and Linux internals. I like low-level work: debugging, understanding how systems actually work, finding where they break (muhahaha).

Since we already established that I'm a dopamine junkie, it's easy to find the flag and skip happily toward a brand new challenge without pausing and actually solidifying all the new knowledge I gained as a side effect from solving the challenges. What a waste.

Cause that's the best way to understand something: by trying to explain it to another person (no bull). So it's a win win for me and for you, dear reader. Hope you enjoy reading the writeups as much as I enjoyed writing them.
